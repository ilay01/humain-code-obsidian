Purpose & Mission

Nova Assistant

Built to provide accessible, conversational AI support for everyday tasks — from drafting emails and summarizing documents to answering research questions. Aims to reduce friction in knowledge work.

MedScan AI

Designed to assist radiologists in detecting early-stage tumors in CT and MRI scans. Acts as a second pair of eyes — flags areas of concern for human review. Never makes final diagnoses alone.

💡 Key Difference

Nova is a general-purpose tool anyone can use. MedScan is a specialized medical tool used only by trained radiologists. This fundamentally changes how each should be evaluated — a medical AI needs stricter safety standards, while a general-purpose AI needs broader fairness testing.

💡 Why MedScan scores higher

Medical AI products face regulatory requirements (FDA, EU MDR) that force higher standards in bias auditing, safety, and privacy. This doesn't mean Nova is "bad" — it means the bar is different for different sectors. A general-purpose chatbot and a tumor-detection system shouldn't be held to identical standards.

Known Limitations

Nova Assistant

●Not for medical/legal/financial advice
●Weaker in non-English languages
●Cannot access real-time data

●Only CT and MRI scans (no X-ray/ultrasound)
●Validated for lung/liver tumors only
●Lower accuracy for pediatric patients
●Requires human radiologist review
●Not validated on scanner models before 2018
